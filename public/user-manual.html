1. Introduction

ASSETLINQ is a full-featured Enterprise Resource Planning system built for Simbisa Brands. It centralises the entire lifecycle of physical and software assets — from procurement and allocation through to maintenance, depreciation, and disposal — and integrates a complete procurement chain covering CAPEX approvals, purchase orders, goods receipts, and invoice matching.

CategoryCapability
Asset ManagementFull asset register with photos, depreciation, QR labels
ProcurementCAPEX → PO → GR → Invoice 3-way matching
OperationsTransfers, allocations, maintenance, software licences
ComplianceAudit trail, activity logs, decommission & disposal records
ReportingPDF, CSV, Sage accounting export
SecurityRole-based access + optional two-factor authentication
System Requirements A modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox) with JavaScript enabled. No software installation required for end users.

2. Getting Started

2.1 Logging In

  1. Navigate to the application URL provided by your administrator.
  2. Enter your email address and password.
  3. Click Log In. If your account has Two-Factor Authentication enabled, you will be redirected to the verification screen (see §2.2).
Locked Out? Use "Forgot your password?" on the login page to receive a reset link by email. Contact your administrator if your account is deactivated.

2.2 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second layer of security by requiring a time-based one-time code from an authenticator app after your password is accepted.

Setting Up 2FA

  1. Log in to ASSETLINQ.
  2. In the sidebar, go to Settings → Two-Factor Auth.
  3. Open any authenticator app on your phone (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy).
  4. Tap + / Add account, then Scan QR code.
  5. Point your camera at the QR code displayed on screen.
  6. Enter the 6-digit code shown in your app into the Confirm Setup field, then click Enable 2FA.
Can't scan? Click "Can't scan? Show the secret key" and manually type the key into your authenticator app instead.

Logging In With 2FA Active

After entering your password you will see a verification card. Open your authenticator app, find ASSETLINQ, and enter the current 6-digit code. Codes refresh every 30 seconds.

Disabling 2FA

Go to Settings → Two-Factor Auth, scroll to the Disable 2FA section, enter your current password, and click Disable 2FA.

3. Asset Register (Dashboard)

The Asset Register is your central view of every asset in the organisation. Access it from Main → Asset Register in the sidebar.

3.1 Viewing Assets

The dashboard displays a table of all assets with columns for Name, Barcode, Serial Number, Category, Department, Status, Condition, and Purchase details. Use the search bar at the top to filter by any keyword across all fields.

3.2 Adding a New Asset admin

  1. Click the + Add Asset button (top-right of the dashboard).
  2. Fill in the asset details: Name, Category, Department, Serial Number, Barcode, Condition, Purchase Date, Purchase Cost, etc.
  3. Optionally upload a photo and set the depreciation method (Straight-Line or Declining Balance), useful life, and salvage value.
  4. Click Save Asset.
Automatic Asset Creation: Assets can also be created automatically when a Goods Receipt is marked as Complete — no manual entry required for purchased equipment.

3.3 Editing an Asset admin

Click the pencil icon in the Actions column for any asset row to open the Edit modal. Make your changes and click Update Asset.

4. Asset Transfers

Use transfers to move assets between departments. Access via Main → Asset Transfers.

4.1 Requesting a Transfer

  1. On the Transfers page, click New Transfer.
  2. Select the asset, the destination department, and provide a reason.
  3. Submit — an admin will be notified to approve or reject.

4.2 Bulk Transfer

On the Dashboard, select multiple assets using their checkboxes, then click Bulk Transfer to move them to a new department in a single action.

4.3 Approving / Rejecting admin

Pending transfers appear on the Transfers page. Click Approve or Reject and the asset's department will update automatically.

5. Asset Allocations

Allocations assign a specific asset to an individual user. Access via Main → Asset Allocations admin.

5.1 Allocating an Asset

  1. Click Allocate Asset.
  2. Choose the asset, the user, and optionally set an allocation date.
  3. Click Allocate.

5.2 Returning an Asset

Find the allocation in the table and click Return. The asset is marked as available again.

6. Asset Requests

Any user can submit a request for a new asset that needs to be procured. Access via Main → Asset Requests.

6.1 Submitting a Request all users

  1. Click New Request.
  2. Fill in the asset type, justification, priority, and required date.
  3. Submit — admins will be notified by email.

6.2 Approving / Declining a Request admin

In the Asset Requests table, click Approve to begin a CAPEX/procurement workflow or Decline to reject with notification to the requester. IT requests are separated into the IT Asset Requests view.

6.3 Export

Click Export CSV on the Asset Requests page to download a full list.

7. CAPEX Forms admin

CAPEX (Capital Expenditure) forms are used to formally request approval for large purchases. They follow a multi-stage email approval workflow. Access via Procurement → CAPEX Forms.

7.1 Creating a CAPEX Form

  1. Click New CAPEX.
  2. Complete the form: description, category, estimated cost, department, linked asset request, and approval chain positions.
  3. Submit — the first approver in the chain is emailed a secure approval link.

7.2 Approval Workflow

Each CAPEX form moves through configurable approval stages. Approvers receive an email with a unique link. They can Approve or Decline directly from their email without logging in. When all stages are approved, vendor quote requests are sent automatically.

7.3 Downloading a CAPEX PDF

From the CAPEX Forms table, click the PDF icon to download a formatted PDF of the form including all approval stages and unit pricing.

8. Purchase Orders admin

Access via Procurement → Purchase Orders.

8.1 Creating a PO

  1. Click New Purchase Order.
  2. Select the vendor, link to a CAPEX form (optional), and add line items with quantities and unit prices.
  3. Totals (excl. VAT, VAT 15%, incl. VAT) are calculated automatically.
  4. Save — the vendor receives a notification email with the PO details; the IT/Finance team also receive copies.

8.2 Tracking Delivery Status

Each PO shows its Delivery Status (Pending / Partial / Complete) and Invoice Status (Unpaid / Paid), which update automatically as goods receipts and invoices are recorded.

8.3 PO PDF

Click the PDF icon on any PO row to download a formatted purchase order document.

9. Goods Receipts admin

Record the physical delivery of goods against a Purchase Order. Access via Procurement → Goods Receipts.

9.1 Recording a Receipt

  1. Click Record Receipt.
  2. Select the Purchase Order from the dropdown — line items are pre-filled.
  3. Enter the quantities actually received for each line item.
  4. Set the delivery status: Partial (some items received) or Complete (all items received).
  5. Click Save Receipt.
Auto Asset Creation: When a receipt is marked Complete, ASSETLINQ automatically creates new asset records for each received item, assigned to the department on the linked CAPEX form.

10. Invoices & Payments admin

Access via Procurement → Invoices & Payments.

10.1 Recording an Invoice

  1. Click Record Invoice.
  2. Select the linked Purchase Order, enter the invoice number, net amount, VAT amount, and invoice date.
  3. Click Save Invoice.

10.2 Three-Way Matching

ASSETLINQ automatically compares the invoice net amount against the PO total. If a discrepancy greater than R0.01 is detected, the invoice is flagged with a ⚠ Mismatch badge. Matching invoices show a ✓ Match badge.

Mismatch detected? Contact your vendor to issue a corrected invoice or credit note before marking as paid.

10.3 Marking an Invoice as Paid

Click Mark Paid on the invoice row. The PO invoice status updates to Paid automatically.

11. Budget vs. Actual admin

Access via Procurement → Budget vs. Actual. This dashboard compares approved CAPEX budgets against actual spend from Purchase Orders, giving a clear view of budget utilisation by department and category.

12. Audit & Inventory

Run physical verification exercises to confirm assets are where the register says they are. Access via Operations → Audit & Inventory.

12.1 Verifying an Asset

  1. Scan or type the asset's barcode or serial number into the search field.
  2. The system finds the asset and displays its registered details.
  3. Confirm the physical location matches. Click Verify to log the audit event.

All verifications are recorded in the activity log with a timestamp and the user who performed the verification.

13. Maintenance Tracking

Access via Operations → Maintenance Tracking.

13.1 Logging a Maintenance Event

  1. Find the asset on the Dashboard or Maintenance page.
  2. Click + Add Maintenance.
  3. Select the type (Preventive / Corrective), enter a description, cost, technician name, and dates.
  4. While under maintenance, the asset status changes to Under Maintenance.
  5. When complete, click Mark Complete — the asset returns to Active.

13.2 Preventive Maintenance Scheduler

Assets with a configured maintenance interval (in days) have their next maintenance date tracked automatically. The system runs a daily check at 06:00 and creates scheduled maintenance records for any assets due for service.

13.3 Warranty Reminders

The system sends automated email alerts to admins and department managers at 30 days and 7 days before an asset's warranty expiry date. No manual action is required.

14. Software Licences admin

Track software licences centrally to control costs and compliance. Access via Operations → Software Licences.

14.1 Adding a Licence

  1. Click + Add Licence.
  2. Complete the form: software name, vendor, licence key, type (Perpetual / Subscription / Per-Seat), seat count, purchase and expiry dates, costs, and status.
  3. Click Save.

14.2 Expiry Alerts

  • ⚠ Expiring Soon — licence expires within 60 days
  • ✕ Expired — licence has passed its expiry date

14.3 Seat Tracking

For Per-Seat licences, update Seats Used as users are added or removed. The table shows available seats at a glance.

15. Asset Lifecycle

Manage the end-of-life stages of assets through Decommission, Disposal, and Archive workflows.

15.1 Decommissioning an Asset admin

From the Dashboard Actions column, click Decommission. The asset status changes to Decommissioned and a record is added to the Decommission Log (Operations → Decommission Log).

15.2 Disposing of an Asset admin

Click Dispose on a decommissioned asset. This moves it to the Disposal Log (Operations → Disposal Certificates) with a formal disposal certificate record.

15.3 Archiving & Restoring

Use Archive to soft-delete an asset without permanently removing it. Archived assets appear in Settings → Archive Utilities, where they can be Restored back to active status.

17. Activity Log & Audit Trail

Access via Reports & Analytics → Activity Log.

17.1 Viewing the Log

All create, update, and delete events across the system are recorded here with timestamp, the user who made the change, and what changed. Use the search bar to filter by description or username, and the Event dropdown to filter by type (Created / Updated / Deleted).

17.2 Per-Asset History

For rows where the subject is an Asset, click View history → or use the 🕐 View History button on the Dashboard to open a full timeline view of every change made to that specific asset — including what fields changed and their before/after values.

18. Executive Summary admin

Access via Reports & Analytics → Executive Summary. This dashboard provides a high-level overview of the asset portfolio including total asset value, spend by category, maintenance costs, and budget utilisation — designed for management reporting.

19. Department Rollup

Access via Reports & Analytics → Department Rollup. This view shows a breakdown of assets, their total value, and maintenance costs grouped by department — useful for internal cost allocation and departmental budgeting reviews.

20. Settings — Administration

Admin Only: All Settings pages are restricted to users with the admin role, except the Two-Factor Auth page which is available to all users.

20.1 User Management

Access via Settings → User Management. Create, edit, activate/deactivate, and delete user accounts.

  • Role: Set to admin or user
  • Active: Deactivating a user prevents login without deleting their data
  • Approval Position: Used in CAPEX approval chains (e.g. "Finance Manager", "CFO")

20.2 Department Management

Access via Settings → Departments. Add, edit, or delete departments.

  • Click Add Department to open the creation modal.
  • Assign a Department Manager (optional) — this user receives warranty reminder emails for assets in their department.
  • Departments with assigned users or assets cannot be deleted — reassign them first.

20.3 Vendor Management

Access via Settings → Vendors. Maintain a central vendor directory. When creating a Purchase Order, vendor details (email, address) are auto-filled from this registry.

20.4 Position Specifications

Access via Settings → Position Specifications. Define standard asset entitlements by job position (e.g. "Senior Manager gets a laptop + mobile phone"). Used to guide asset allocation decisions.

20.5 Archive Utilities

Access via Settings → Archive Utilities. View all soft-archived assets and restore them to active status if needed.

20.6 Two-Factor Auth Setup

Access via Settings → Two-Factor Auth. Available to all users. See §2.2 for full setup instructions.

21. QR Code Labels

ASSETLINQ can generate a printable PDF label for any asset containing a QR code, barcode, key details, and warranty information — ideal for label printers or standard A4 printing.

21.1 Printing a Label

  1. On the Asset Register, find the asset you need to label.
  2. Click the 🖨 Print QR Label button (purple printer icon) in the Actions column.
  3. A PDF opens in a new browser tab (approx. 80mm × 100mm label size).
  4. Print using your browser's print dialogue. For label printers, set paper size to match your labels.

The label includes: Company header, QR code (encoding the asset barcode), asset name, barcode number, serial number, department, location, warranty expiry date, and print date.

21.2 Scanning the QR Code

The QR code encodes the asset's barcode number. Scan it with any QR scanner app or the audit module's search field to instantly pull up the asset record.

22. Troubleshooting

IssueWhat to do
Cannot log inUse "Forgot Password" to reset. If still blocked, contact your administrator — your account may be deactivated.
2FA code rejectedEnsure your phone's clock/time is correct (TOTP is time-sensitive). Try the next code (they refresh every 30 s).
Lost access to authenticator appContact your administrator. They can disable 2FA on your account from User Management.
Invoice shows ⚠ MismatchThe invoice amount differs from the PO by more than R0.01. Verify both figures and contact the vendor for a corrected document.
Cannot delete a departmentThe department has users or assets linked to it. Reassign them first, then retry the deletion.
Asset not found in audit scanCheck the barcode/serial matches exactly what's on record. The asset may have been archived — check Archive Utilities.
CAPEX email approval not receivedCheck the approver's spam folder. Verify the approver has a valid email and the correct approval position is assigned to them in Settings → Users.
QR label PDF is blankEnsure the asset has a barcode number saved. Edit the asset and add a barcode, then retry printing the label.
Reports page shows no dataEnsure there is data in the relevant module (e.g. no assets → Asset CSV will be empty). Check with your administrator.
Need further help? For system issues not covered here, contact your ASSETLINQ system administrator or the IT support team.
